Structure Topic sentence: The first sentence of a paragraph that introduces the main idea. It's also known as the focus sentence. Supporting sentences: Sentences that provide examples or explanations related to the topic sentence. Concluding sentence: The final sentence of a paragraph. Types of paragraphs Descriptive paragraphs: Use figurative language to appeal to the reader's senses and imagination. They are often used in creative writing and fiction. Persuasive paragraphs: Aim to convince the reader of a point or opinion. Academic paragraphs: Use evidence to support a specific claim. They are usually well-developed and longer than other paragraphs. Compare and contrast paragraphs: Examine similarities and differences. Purpose Paragraphs help organize ideas and build an argument. They break up text into manageable units so that the reader can easily see the main sections. Paragraphs inform and entertain the reader about the paper's overall idea.
